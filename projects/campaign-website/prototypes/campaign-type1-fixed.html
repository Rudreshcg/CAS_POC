Finding 01

Your soda ash margins are absorbing Chinese import pressure that your contracts aren't indexed to deflect

Global soda ash prices have declined 18% over the past 14 months, driven by expanded Chinese production capacity. Apollo's analysis of Tata Chemicals' procurement contracts indicates that a significant portion of your domestic soda ash purchases are on fixed-price annual contracts — meaning you are not capturing the downward price movement that your competitors with indexed contracts are already benefiting from.

Apollo's Should-Cost Modeling agent builds a live bottom-up cost model for soda ash that reflects current Chinese export pricing, energy input costs, and logistics — giving your negotiating team a defensible number to bring to the next contract renewal.

💼 Estimated CFO impact: Transitioning 40% of fixed-price soda ash contracts to indexed structures could represent ₹28–45Cr in annual cost reduction at current market rates.
Finding 02

Your specialty chemical supplier base has three single-source dependencies that create material supply risk

Apollo's analysis of Tata Chemicals' specialty chemical procurement identified three material categories — sodium bicarbonate precursors, specific chlorine derivatives, and silica compounds — where your supply base shows single-source concentration. Two of these suppliers are in geographies currently experiencing logistics volatility.

Apollo's Alternate Suppliers agent continuously scans global trade databases and qualified supplier networks to identify pre-vetted alternative sources — complete with landed cost comparisons, quality certification status, and lead time profiles — before a supply disruption forces an emergency decision.

👤 Estimated CPO impact: Qualifying two alternate sources per single-source category reduces emergency sourcing premium exposure — historically 15–30% above contracted price during supply events.
